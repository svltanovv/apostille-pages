ABOUT E-APOSTILLE GLOBAL

Apostille and Legalization e-Register

The Apostille and Legalization e-Register was developed to combat fraud and abuse of the apostille or legalization certificate and further modification of the underlying public document. With the Apostille and Legalization e-Register, any interested party can verify the fact of apostillization or legalization of a document, visually verify the Apostille or Legalization Certificate, and view the scanned version of the underlying document.

The growing adoption of the electronic apostille (e-Apostille) marks a significant advance in international document authentication. As more countries and authorities adopt this digital solution, the process of verifying documents for global use becomes increasingly streamlined and efficient. The e-Apostille system provides a secure and reliable method for authenticating a wide range of documents, from personal certificates to legal documents, thereby facilitating smoother international interactions.

Despite its growing popularity, it is crucial to recognize that the adoption of the e-Apostille is not yet universal, and the states parties to the convention have historically maintained electronic registers disjointedly.

The introduction of a unified E-APOSTILLE GLOBAL information system, which performs the functions of interacting with all registers of the states parties to the convention with the ability to verify and legalize documents, is a revolutionary step towards the digitalization and simplification of international document legalization.

* To connect to the system, you must contact the system operator.

* To verify documents issued before January 15, 2026, you must contact the national electronic apostille register of the country that legalized the document.

* In countries that have adopted this system, there may still be specific requesting authorities or institutions that are unfamiliar with e-Apostille or hesitant to accept it. This difference in adoption levels highlights the importance of understanding the specific requirements of the destination country or authority.
